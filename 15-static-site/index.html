Experts Corner

From Medina to Modernity: Marrakech’s Living Galleries

Between spice-scented souks and riad courtyards, Marrakech preserves Amazigh craft lineages that inform today’s Moroccan contemporary art—zellige geometry, tadelakt surfaces, and indigo pathways.

Nilotic Narratives: Cairo and the Afterlives of Antiquity

Egyptian ateliers reinterpret pharaonic forms—falcons, lotuses, and cartouches—through glass, metal, and textile, bridging dynastic memory with cosmopolitan Cairo.

City in Motion: Lagos, Sound, and Sculpture

From Oshodi’s pulse to Victoria Island salons, Lagos artists fuse Afrobeats rhythm with bronze-casting heritage from Benin to create objects that hum with urban cadence.

Coastlines of Memory: Dakar to Cape Town

Senegal’s École de Dakar and South Africa’s post-apartheid studios explore identity and materiality—textiles, found-wood, and light—mapping personal geographies across the Atlantic edge.
